roteasy.src.roteasy_lib.rotation_matrix(angles=(), axes=’‘)[source]¶

Core function to obtain the final rotation matrix \(R\).

\[R(\theta_1,\theta_2,....,\theta_N)=R_1(\theta_1) \cdot R_2(\theta_2) \cdot ..... \cdot R_N(\theta_N)\]

where \(\theta_i\) is the anticlockwise rotation angle around the axis \(x_i\) e.g.:

angles=(30,60,80)
axes='xyz'

R=rotation_matrix(angles=angles,axes=axes)

In this case R is the rotation matrix representing a rotation of 30 degree around the x axis, then a rotation of 60 degree around the new y axis and finally a rotation of 80 degree around the final z axis.

Parameters:
  • angles – (anticlockwise) rotation angles (list, tuple or numpy array).
  • axes – str representing the rotation axes.
Returns:

final rotation matrix (see above).
roteasy.src.roteasy_lib.rotation_matrix_x(angle)[source]¶

Calculate the matrix that defines the anticlockwise rotation of a point around the x axis:

\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos \theta & \sin \theta \\ 0 & -\sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix}
Parameters:angle – (anticlockwise) rotation angle ( \(\theta\)) in degree
Returns:a 3x3 numpy array containing the rotation matrix (see above).
